Registration

  • Registration for the CBE7 is now processed through PayPal.
  • Registration fees are in Brazilian Reais (BRL - R$) and includes all coffee breaks and lunches, and cocktails at poster sessions.
  • We are offering discounted registration rates for current members of the Deep-Sea Biology Society (DSBS). If you are not a member, we encourage you to become one (https://dsbsoc.wildapricot.org/) to be eligible for discounted rates and travel awards. Becoming a member and registering as a DSBS member can save you between R$ 300-350 or equivalent to US$ 70-80 (depending on your membership eligibility), when compared to registering as a non-member.
  • The 'Student' entry category includes undergraduate, master's and doctoral students. The 'Professional' entry category includes Postdoctoral researchers, Technicians, Researchers, Professors and others.
  • IMPORTANT: if you are paying registration fees on behalf of someone else, please do send us a message at cbe7brazil@gmail.com indicating the who does this registration corresponds to.

Abstract Submission

Guidelines and Instructions

  • The abstract submission deadline is 31 May 2023, 23:59 pm BRT (Brasília Time).
  • Abstracts must be submitted electronically and in English, for oral (15 minutes) or poster presentation.
  • Abstract title is limited to 15 words. Abstract text is limited to 500 words.
  • You will need to indicate your 1-2 top session topic choices for your abstract. Please read session descriptions below.
  • You can submit more than one abstract as presenter, however there will be a limit of one talk presentation per presenter (Each abstract will be evaluated independently. There are no guarantees that by submitting multiple abstracts one will be selected as a talk).
  • Please fill the form below and upload your abstract (in .pdf, and maximum 10 MB) following the template.

Session Topics

Abstracts summarizing new advances in chemosynthesis-based ecosystems will be considered for oral or poster presentations in the following topical areas:

  1. Diversity and community structure and dynamics
  2. Trophic ecology – importance of chemosynthesis
  3. Biogeochemistry, geochemistry and geology
  4. Biogeography, Evolutionary history and paleo
  5. Microbial ecology – diversity and function
  6. Symbiosis
  7. Chemosynthesis-based Ecosystems in the perspective of the Decade of Ocean Science for Sustainable Development
  8. Others

Cancellation Policy

Registration fees are fully refundable (minus any processing fees) until May 31, 2023. Registration fees are 50% refundable (minus any processing fees) until June 30, 2023. Registration fees are not refundable after June 30, 2023.
